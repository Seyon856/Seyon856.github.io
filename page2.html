Steph Curry's College Career

Freshman Season

In his freshman season (2006–07), Curry had several highlight games, including a 32-point, 9-rebound outing vs. Michigan and a 30-point, 11-rebound outing vs. Chattanooga for his first career double-double. He broke the school's 500-point freshmen record with 730 points and Davidson's single-season three-pointer record. Curry led the Southern Conference in scoring (21.5 PPG), ranking ninth overall and second in freshmen behind only Kevin Durant. Davidson finished the season 29–5, winning the Southern Conference regular-season championship. Curry also set the NCAA freshman season record for three-pointers (113) and scored 30 points in his first-round NCAA tournament game against Maryland. He received numerous awards, including SoCon Freshman of the Year, SoCon Tournament Most Valuable Player, All-SoCon First Team, and various all-freshman and all-tournament honors.
