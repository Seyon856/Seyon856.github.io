Steph Curry's College Career

Freshman Season

In his freshman season (2006–07), Curry had several highlight games, including a 32-point, 9-rebound outing vs. Michigan and a 30-point, 11-rebound outing vs. Chattanooga for his first career double-double. He broke the school's 500-point freshmen record with 730 points and Davidson's single-season three-pointer record. Curry led the Southern Conference in scoring (21.5 PPG), ranking ninth overall and second in freshmen behind only Kevin Durant. Davidson finished the season 29–5, winning the Southern Conference regular-season championship. Curry also set the NCAA freshman season record for three-pointers (113) and scored 30 points in his first-round NCAA tournament game against Maryland. He received numerous awards, including SoCon Freshman of the Year, SoCon Tournament Most Valuable Player, All-SoCon First Team, and various all-freshman and all-tournament honors.

Sophomore Season

In the 2007–08 season, Steph Curry led Davidson to a 26–6 regular-season record, a perfect 20–0 conference record, and the NCAA tournament. He scored 40 points (including 8-of-10 three-pointers) in a comeback win over Gonzaga, 25 second-half points to beat Georgetown, and 33 points against Wisconsin to go to the Elite Eight. In the Final Four game against #1 Kansas, Curry broke the NCAA single season three-point record, making his 159th three that season. However, they came up short, losing 59-57. Curry became one of the few college players to score over 30 points in his first four NCAA tournament games. He finished the season averaging 25.9 points, 2.9 assists, and 2.1 steals per game. Curry earned AP All-America Second Team honors, was named Midwest Region Most Outstanding Player, and received an ESPY nomination for Breakthrough Player of the Year.

Junior Season

In his junior year, Curry had several great performances, including two career-high 44-point games and a 30-point, 13-assist game against Winthrop. He passed 2,000 career points on January 3, 2009, and became Davidson’s all-time leading scorer with 2,488 points on February 28. Davidson won the Southern Conference South Division with an 18–2 record but lost in the conference tournament semifinals. The team did not receive an NCAA bid, instead playing in the NIT, where Curry scored 32 points against South Carolina and 26 points with nine rebounds and five assists in his final college game versus Saint Mary’s. Curry finished the season averaging 28.6 points, 5.6 assists, and 2.5 steals per game, was the NCAA scoring champion, and earned first-team All-American honors before entering the 2009 NBA Draft.
